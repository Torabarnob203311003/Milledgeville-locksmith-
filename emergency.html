Smart Key Tips

Wet Smart Keys & Push-To-Start Problems

Modern push-to-start vehicles depend heavily on smart key communication. When a smart key becomes water damaged, customers sometimes continue pressing the buttons repeatedly — unfortunately, that can sometimes create additional internal damage.

💧 If A Smart Key Gets Wet
  1. Remove the emergency key immediately
  2. Open the remote as soon as possible
  3. Remove the battery immediately
  4. Allow the remote to dry naturally
  5. Avoid direct heat whenever possible

The faster the battery is removed, the better the chance of reducing internal corrosion damage inside the remote. In some situations, the remote may still be saved before complete failure happens.

⚠️

Do NOT Keep Pressing Buttons

A lot of people continue pressing the buttons repeatedly hoping the remote will suddenly work again. Unfortunately, continued button pressing can sometimes create additional internal damage to the remote electronics.

If the smart key gets wet, remove the battery as quickly as possible and call for assistance.
